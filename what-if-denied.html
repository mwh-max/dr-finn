Denied

What If Denied?

A denial is not necessarily the end. You have the right to appeal, and many denials are overturned.

Read the Denial Letter Carefully

Your denial letter will explain the reason for the denial. Common reasons include income too high, missing documents, or a verification issue. Sometimes a denial is simply a paperwork problem that can be fixed quickly.

How to Appeal

You have 30 days from the date on your denial letter to file an appeal. You can request a State Fair Hearing by:

  • Calling FSSA: 800-403-0864
  • Writing to: FSSA Office of Administrative Law Proceedings, 402 W. Washington St., Indianapolis, IN 46204
  • Visiting your local DFR office

Can You Reapply?

Yes. If your situation has changed, you can reapply at any time. There is no waiting period between applications.

Get Help

  • Indiana Legal Services — free civil legal aid: indianalegalservices.org
  • Your local DFR office — find one near you
