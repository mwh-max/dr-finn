Glossary

Medicaid paperwork is full of abbreviations and jargon. Here is what they mean in plain English.

ABD Medicaid

Coverage for people who are Aged (65+), Blind, or Disabled. Different income and asset rules than HIP.

Co-pay

A small amount you pay when you use a service â€” like $3 for a doctor visit. On HIP, co-pays come from your POWER account, not your wallet directly.

FPL (Federal Poverty Level)

A number the federal government sets each year based on income and household size. Indiana Medicaid uses FPL to decide if you qualify. For example, HIP covers adults up to 138% of the FPL.

FSSA

Family and Social Services Administration â€” the Indiana state agency that runs Medicaid. FSSA oversees DFR offices and the FSSA Benefits Helpline (800-403-0864).

Indiana's Medicaid program for children under 19, pregnant women, and some low-income families.

MCO (Managed Care Organization)

A private health insurance company that Indiana pays to manage your Medicaid benefits. Indiana's MCOs are MDwise, Anthem, Indiana Total Care, and CareSource. Your MCO issues your Medicaid ID card.

NEMT

Non-Emergency Medical Transportation â€” free rides to medical appointments for Medicaid members who cannot otherwise get there. Arranged through your MCO.

PCP (Primary Care Provider)

Your main doctor â€” the one you see for checkups and who refers you to specialists. Every Medicaid member is assigned or chooses a PCP.

POWER Account

A health savings account used by HIP Plus members. You contribute a small monthly amount; Indiana adds more. Co-pays come from this account. If you stop contributing, you drop to HIP Basic.

Prior Authorization

Approval your MCO requires before covering certain services or medications. Your doctor requests this on your behalf. Without it, the cost may not be covered.
