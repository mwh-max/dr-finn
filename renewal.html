Renewal

Indiana Medicaid renews every 12 months. FSSA will contact you before your coverage ends. Do not ignore that notice.

When Does Renewal Happen?

FSSA reviews your eligibility once per year. About 60â€“90 days before your renewal date, you will receive a notice by mail or through your gateway.in.gov account.

Make sure your address on file is current. If FSSA cannot reach you, your coverage may end.

What FSSA Does First

Indiana first tries to renew your coverage automatically using data they already have â€” from tax records, Social Security, and other sources.

If they can confirm you still qualify, you may not need to do anything. You will get a notice saying your coverage was renewed.

When You Need to Act

If FSSA cannot renew you automatically, they will send a renewal packet asking you to confirm or update your information. You typically have 30 days to respond.

You can renew:

  • Online at gateway.in.gov
  • By phone: 800-403-0864
  • By mail: return the form in the envelope provided
  • In person at a DFR office

What If You Miss the Deadline?

If you do not respond in time, your coverage will end. However, you can often get it reinstated quickly if you act within a short window â€” call FSSA as soon as possible.

If your coverage has already ended, you will need to reapply.
